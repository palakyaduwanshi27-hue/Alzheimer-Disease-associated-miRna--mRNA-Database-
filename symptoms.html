Alzheimer's Disease

Symptoms & Diagnosis

Common Symptoms

Alzheimer’s disease affects memory, thinking, and behavior. Symptoms may develop gradually and worsen over time.

  • Memory loss affecting daily life
  • Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  • Confusion with time and place
  • Challenges in problem-solving
  • Difficulty in speaking or writing
  • Changes in mood, personality, or behavior

Diagnosis of Alzheimer's

There is no single test for Alzheimer’s disease. Diagnosis involves multiple assessments, including:

  • Medical history and cognitive tests
  • Brain imaging (MRI, CT scan, PET scan)
  • Neurological exams
  • Blood tests to rule out other conditions
  • Psychological evaluations
